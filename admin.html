📖 How to Write Articles That Rank on Google

Follow these guidelines every day for AdSense approval and Google ranking.

🎯 Write one article per day, minimum 400 words

Google rewards fresh content. One article daily on IPL 2026 — match reports, previews, player features, squad news — is enough to get indexed and ranked. Quality beats quantity. 400-600 words is the sweet spot.

📰 Article types that drive Google traffic

Match Previews (post the night before): "DC vs GT Match 14 Preview: Pitch Report, Probable XI, Head to Head" — these get searched the morning of every match.

Match Reports (post within 1 hour of match): "RR beat MI by 27 runs: Jaiswal 77* Steers Rain-Hit Victory — Match 13 Report"

Player Features: "Vaibhav Sooryavanshi: Why IPL 2026's 15-Year-Old Sensation Is Already Special"

Analysis: "Why CSK Are Struggling: 3 Tactical Problems Sanju Samson Must Fix"

✍️ Article structure that Google likes

<h2> Quick Match Summary (or main topic heading)
2-3 paragraphs of original analysis
<h2> Key Performances / Key Analysis
2-3 more paragraphs
<h2> What This Means / Looking Ahead
1-2 concluding paragraphs

Always link to iplt20.com for official stats — this signals credibility to Google.

🔑 Title formula that gets clicks

Match report: [Team] beat [Team] by [margin]: [Star player] [performance] — Match [N] Report
Example: "RR Beat MI by 27 Runs: Jaiswal's Unbeaten 77 Seals Rain-Hit Guwahati Thriller"

Preview: [Team A] vs [Team B] Preview: Pitch Report, Probable XI and Key Battles — Match [N]

Feature: [Player Name]: [Compelling Angle] — [Why it Matters]

🚫 What NOT to do (AdSense rules)

❌ Do NOT copy text from Cricbuzz or ESPNcricinfo — Google will detect it and penalise you
❌ Do NOT write less than 300 words — Google won't rank thin content
❌ Do NOT use click-bait titles that don't match the article
❌ Do NOT publish the same information twice under different titles
✅ Always write in your own words, from your own analysis
✅ Use facts (scores, names) but describe them in your own perspective
