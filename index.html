IPL 2026 — Complete Season Guide

The TATA Indian Premier League 2026 is the 19th edition of the world's most-watched T20 cricket tournament. Running from March 28 to May 31, 2026, it features 10 franchises battling across 74 matches — 70 league games and 4 playoffs — with the IPL 2026 Final in Bengaluru on May 31.

Key Storylines This Season

Defending champions RCB are chasing back-to-back titles. Teenage sensation Vaibhav Sooryavanshi has taken IPL 2026 by storm for Rajasthan Royals. Cameron Green became the most expensive overseas player in IPL history at ₹25.20 crore (KKR). Sanju Samson switched from RR to CSK while Ravindra Jadeja went the other way.

How to Watch IPL 2026

Live on Star Sports (TV) and JioHotstar (digital streaming) in India. International viewers: Willow TV (North America) and Sky Sports (UK).

IPL 2026 Points System

Each win = 2 points. No result/rain = 1 point. Loss = 0 points. The top 4 teams after 70 league matches qualify for playoffs. When teams are tied on points, Net Run Rate (NRR) is the tiebreaker.

How the IPL 2026 Points System Works

Each win earns a team 2 points. A match abandoned or washed out due to rain earns both teams 1 point each. A loss earns 0 points. After all 70 league matches, the top 4 teams qualify for the playoffs.

What is NRR?

Net Run Rate = (Total runs scored ÷ Total overs faced) – (Total runs conceded ÷ Total overs bowled). A positive NRR means you're scoring faster than opponents score against you. NRR is the tiebreaker when teams finish level on points — so big wins matter.

IPL 2026 Playoff Format

Qualifier 1: 1st vs 2nd — winner goes directly to the Final. Eliminator: 3rd vs 4th — loser is eliminated. Qualifier 2: Loser of Q1 vs winner of Eliminator. Final: May 31, 2026 in Bengaluru.

IPL 2026 Schedule & Venues

IPL 2026 runs from March 28 to May 31, 2026, spanning 14 stadiums across India — the widest geographical spread in IPL history. Punjab Kings split home games between New Chandigarh and Dharamsala. Rajasthan Royals use Guwahati and Jaipur. RCB play in Bengaluru and Raipur.

Phase 1 vs Phase 2

The BCCI split the schedule into two phases due to upcoming assembly elections in West Bengal, Tamil Nadu, and Assam. Phase 1: Matches 1–20 (March 28–April 12). Phase 2: Matches 21–74 (April 13–May 31).

IPL 2026 Playoffs

Qualifier 1, Eliminator, and Qualifier 2 venues will be announced separately. The IPL 2026 Final is on May 31 in Bengaluru.

IPL 2026 Team Guide

The 2026 Indian Premier League features the same 10 franchises from 2025, but with significant changes across squads and captaincy. Here's the full team breakdown:

Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB)

Defending IPL 2026 champions. Home ground: M Chinnaswamy Stadium, Bengaluru and Shaheed Veer Narayan Singh Stadium, Raipur.

Chennai Super Kings (CSK)

Five-time champions now captained by Sanju Samson, who joined from Rajasthan Royals. Home: MA Chidambaram Stadium, Chennai.

Mumbai Indians (MI)

Five-time champions. One of the most successful IPL franchises. Home: Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai.

Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR)

Three-time champions who signed Cameron Green for a record ₹25.20 crore — the most expensive overseas player in IPL history. Home: Eden Gardens, Kolkata.

Rajasthan Royals (RR)

Powered by teenage sensation Vaibhav Sooryavanshi and now home to Ravindra Jadeja who joined from CSK. Home grounds: Sawai Mansingh Stadium (Jaipur) and Barsapara Stadium (Guwahati).

Sunrisers Hyderabad (SRH)

Known for aggressive batting and explosive openers. Home: Rajiv Gandhi International Stadium, Hyderabad.

Punjab Kings (PBKS)

Currently leading IPL 2026 standings. Led by Shreyas Iyer. Home: New Chandigarh and Dharamsala.

Gujarat Titans (GT)

Two-time finalist (2022 winners, 2023 runners-up). Home: Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad.

Delhi Capitals (DC)

Led by Axar Patel. Home: Arun Jaitley Stadium, Delhi and Dr. YS Rajasekhara Reddy ACA-VDCA Stadium, Visakhapatnam.

Lucknow Super Giants (LSG)

Franchise entered IPL in 2022. Home: BRSABV Ekana Cricket Stadium, Lucknow.
